Here is a timeline of some of Grace Hooper's achievements:

  • 1906 - Grace is born in New York City
  • 1913 - At the young age of 7, Grace began to take apart family alarm clocks in order to tryn and figure out how they worked.
  • 1928 - Grace graduate Phi Beta Kappa from Vassar College with a bachelor's degree in Mathematics and Physics.
  • 1930 - Grace eaned her Master's degree from Yale.
  • 1931 - She began teaching Mathematics at Vassar.
  • 1934 - Grace earned her Ph.D in Mathematics from Yale.
  • 1941 - Vassar College promotes her to associate professor.
  • 1943 - After initialy being rejected due to her position, Grace took a leave of absence from Vassar in order to join the Navy during WWII.
  • 1944 - Grace graduated top of her class from Naval Reserve Midshipmen's school at Smith College and assigned to the Bureau of Ships Computation Project at Harvard University as a lieutenant, junior grade.
  • 1947 - While working on the Mark II computer at Harvard, Grace and her associates discovered and had to litteraly debug a moth that was impeding the operation of the relay.
  • 1949 - Grace became senior mathemetician at Ecker-Mauchly Computer Corporation and served as director of Aytomatic Programing Development for UNIVAC which was the first large-scale electronic computer on the market in 1950.
    During this time, Grace brought up the idea of using English to create code instead of numbers and symbols. She was told it wouldn't work because computers dont understand English.
  • 1952 - Grace created an operational link-loader. Origionally others rejected it claiming that computers could only do arithmetic.
  • 1954 - Grace was named the company's first director of automatic programing and released some of the first compiler based programing languages such as MATH-MATIC and FLOW=MATIC.
  • 1959 - She served as a technical consultant to the commitee which developed the new coding language COBOL which is still used today.
  • 1970s - Grace advocated for the Defense Department to replace its old, large centrilized systems with networks of small, distributed computers.
  • 1983 - Grace was promoted to the rank of Commodore, which was later renamed to rear admiral.
  • 1986 After serving since WWII, Grace retired from the Navy.
  • 1992 - Grace died in her sleep on New Years Day.
